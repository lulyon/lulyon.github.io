On Universities' Independent Recruitment

2011-12-10 20:20

Recently, some top universities in China have started to recruit students independently. The independent recruitment is regarded as latest attempt to improve China's widely-criticized college recruitment system.

While the independent recruitment is worth trying, according to the director of admission, will encourage innovation significantly, it should not be adopted as the mainstream way of college admission.

Among many of the crucial standards for selecting students, the equality of the selecting process is overwhelmingly critical. That is to say, people should be assured that they compete by a fair way in this far-reaching contest. Without fairness, the following efforts would be in vain.

As a matter of fact, the national entrance examination, though criticized for harming students innovation, which should be better considered as the responsibility of college education rather than that of college admission, is satisfactory for meeting the criteria of equality, which is no doubt the most essential principle of college admission.

On the contrary, the independent recruitment is by no means impartial in current circumstance. Students from rural areas are not as well-informed as other students, since they are not generally aware of the independent recruitment, thus losing the opportunity, and students who occasionally hear about the independent recruitment could not gain access to the preparatory material to prepare for the examination, resulting in poorer performance in the contest. What's more, there is a lack of government regulation for the independent recruitment, which might lead to corruption in the course of recruitment. As a consequence, the social inequality would eventually soar. In addition, the supposed advantage of independent recruitment, which is to enhance innovation, is still unproved.

In conclusion, we are supposed to keep the national entrance examination as a key method for college admission, in the meanwhile giving the independent recruitment a try in a limited scale. Let's wait and see if the independent recruitment would make a difference.
